Chocolate Chip Cookies

Prep Time: 15 mins    Cook Time:12 mins

Total Estimated Time: 3 hours 30 mins

Yield: 16 XL cookies or 20 medium/large cookies

Description

A classic recipe for super soft and chewy chocolate chip cookies, perfect for any occasion.

Ingredients

  • 280g All-Purpose Flour
  • 1 teaspoon Baking Soda
  • 1 and 1/2 teaspoons Cornstarch
  • 1/2 teaspoon Salt
  • 170g Unsalted Butter, melted & cooled 5 minutes
  • 150g Brown Sugar
  • 100g Granulated Sugar
  • 1 Egg & 1Egg Yolk, at room temperature
  • 2 teaspoons Pure Valina Extract
  • 225g Semi-sweet Chocolate Chips

Instructions

  1. Whisk the flour, baking soda, cornstarch, and salt together in a large bowl. Set aside.

  2. In a medium bowl, whisk the melted butter, brown sugar, and granualted sugar together until no brown sugar lumps remain. Whisk in the egg and egg yolk. Finally, whisk in the vanila extract. The mixture will be thin. Pour into dry ingredients and mix together with a large spoon or spatula. The dough will be very soft, thick, and appear greasy. Fold in the chocolate chips. The chocolate chips may not stick to the dough because of the melted butter, but do you best to combine them.

  3. Cover the dough tighly and chill in the refrigerator for at least 2-3 hours or 1 day.I highly reccomend chilling the cookie dough overnight for less spreading and chewier cookies.

  4. Take the dough out of the refrigerator and allow it to slightly soften at room temperature for 10 minutes.

  5. Preheat oven to 325℉ (160℃). Line large baking sheets with parchment paper or silicone baking mats. Set aside.

  6. Using a cookie scoop or Tablespoon measuring spoon, measure 3 Tablespoons (60g) of dough for XL cookies or 2 Tablespoons (50g) of dough for medium/large cookies. Roll into a ball. Repeat with remaining dough. Place 8–9 balls of dough onto each cookie sheet.

  7. Bake the cookies for 12–13 minutes or until the edges are very lightly browned. (XL cookies can take closer to 14 minutes.) The centers will look very soft, but the cookies will continue to set as they cool. Cool on the baking sheet for 10 minutes. Meanwhile, press a few extra chocolate chips into the tops of the warm cookies. This is optional and only for looks. After 10 minutes of cooling on the baking sheets, transfer cookies to a wire rack to cool completely.

  8. Cookies stay fresh covered at room temperature for up to 1 week.

Enjoy your homemade chocolate chip cookies!
